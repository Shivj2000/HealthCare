Osteoporosis

Osteoporosis:


It Includes:

  1. Osteoporosis is one of the most common medical conditions affecting older people. More than three million people across the UK have osteoporosis, with more than 500,000 people receiving hospital treatment for fragility fractures every year as a result. This condition develops slowly over time and is often left undiagnosed until a fall causes a bone fracture.
  2. Women are more likely to have osteoporosis because they lose bone density rapidly after going through menopause. Luckily, certain medications can help to strengthen the bones. Many people also take calcium and vitamin D supplements to maintain bone health.

    3. Symptoms:

    1. Back pain, caused by a fractured or collapsed vertebra
    2. Loss of height over time
    3. A stooped posture
    4. A bone that breaks much more easily than expected

      5. Treatment:

      1. Regular Exercise
      2. Healthy Eating
      3. No Smoking and Less Drinking
      4. Plenty of Sunshine
