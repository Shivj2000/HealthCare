Chronic kidney disease

Chronic kidney disease:


It Includes:

  1. Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is quite common among older people here in the UK. There are several other medical conditions that affect the kidneys and can lead to chronic kidney disease. These conditions include kidney infections, high blood pressure, diabetes and kidney inflammation.
  2. According to Kidney Care UK, around 64,000 people in the UK are receiving treatment for kidney failure – this is stage 5 chronic kidney disease, where kidney function is less than 15%.

    3. Symptoms:

    1. Shortness of breath.
    2. Feeling sick.
    3. Blood in your urine.
    4. Swollen ankles, feet or hands.
    5. Tiredness.

      6. Treatment:

      1. Medication – To control associated problems such as hypertension and high cholesterol.
      2. Lifestyle changes – To ensure that your body remains as healthy as possible.
      3. Dialysis – Replicates some of the kidney’s functions, which may be necessary if your condition is at an advanced stage.
      4. Kidney transplant – May be needed if your condition is at an advanced stage.
